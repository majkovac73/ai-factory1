# Step 100h — Real-quality listing/ad mockups (design composited into scenes) + watermark protection

**Date:** 2026-07-09

## Problem (two, raised by Maj)

1. **Product theft.** Step 100g made the digital listing photos the flat delivered
   design — and worse, the *raw clean deliverable itself was uploaded as the
   primary public listing photo*. Anyone could download the full, usable product
   for free from the listing preview instead of buying it.
2. **Bare product, not an ad.** Even fixed, a flat design (or a flat design +
   watermark) looks like a bare product scan, not the attractive lifestyle ad the
   marketing images used to be. Maj: *"the images have to look as before, not
   just the product with a watermark."*

The tension: attractive ads used to be **independent** generations, which depict a
genuinely different illustration than the delivery (a different dinosaur) and the
consistency gate correctly rejects them. So we need ads that look great **and**
show the true product.

## Fix — composite the REAL design into realistic scenes, then watermark

New `app/services/mockup_service.py` (`MockupService`) — the way real Etsy digital
sellers do it:

- The **empty scene** (a softly-lit blank wall; a desk flat-lay with a plant and
  coffee and empty centre space) is generated by the image provider. It contains
  **no product-specific design**, so it can never misrepresent.
- The **real delivered design** is composited on top with PIL — never
  regenerated: a **framed print** (white matte, dark wooden frame with a bevel,
  soft drop shadow) for the wall scene, and a **flat print** (paper border, slight
  rotation, soft shadow) for the desk flat-lay.
- A **tiled diagonal watermark** is applied across the whole image, so the public
  preview shows exactly what the buyer gets but can't be used as the product (it
  can't be cropped out).
- If scene generation fails (or `MOCKUP_USE_GENERATED_SCENES=False`), a
  deterministic PIL studio/desk-gradient background is used — a mockup is always
  produced.

Because the ad is built **from** the real design, it's guaranteed truthful → no
consistency conflict, and it looks like a professional framed-print / flat-lay ad.

### Security: the clean file is never public

`run_post_completion` (digital single-image, step 2.6) now sets
`image_paths = self._build_listing_mockups(...)` — **only the watermarked mockups**
are public listing photos. The raw, clean, unwatermarked deliverable is uploaded
**only** as the buyer-gated digital FILE (`digital_file_path=design_path` in
`_stage_attach_publish`) — never as a listing photo. New test [6] asserts exactly
this (the clean file is the digital file, and is NOT among the uploaded photos).

### Reused for Tumblr/Pinterest too

The marketing-refresh worker (Tumblr) already posts the catalog's `listing`
assets — which are now these watermarked, real-design composites. So the same fix
gives attractive, protected ads on **both** the Etsy listing and Tumblr, from one
place. (The main-pipeline Pinterest pin still uses an independent 2:3 generation —
a separate top-of-funnel channel, unchanged here.)

### Settings

- `LISTING_WATERMARK_TEXT` (default `"PREVIEW"`) — set to the shop name for a
  branded watermark.
- `MOCKUP_USE_GENERATED_SCENES` (default `True`) — composite into a generated
  photorealistic scene; `False` uses the deterministic PIL background.

## Consistency check

Unchanged and still fully active for POD/PDF (independent listing photos). For
digital single-image it remains **skipped** (step 100g): the photos are built from
the already content-verified delivery, so there is no independent image to verify.
This is correct, not a weakening — a composite of the real design can't
misrepresent it.

## Cost

2 scene generations per digital product (~$0.04 each ≈ $0.08) — the same ballpark
as the 2 independent hero/lifestyle generations they replace. The design composite
and watermark are free (PIL).

## Tests

- **`scripts/test_step100g_delivery_mockups.py`** (6/6): mockups are valid
  1024×1024; **depict the real design composited into a scene** (design dominant in
  the centre, scene at the edges); are **watermarked** (test [5]); registered as
  `listing`; robust to an unreadable delivery; and — critically — test **[6]**: the
  raw clean deliverable is the buyer-gated digital file and is **never** a public
  listing photo (only the watermarked mockups are).
- Full regression green: steps **69, 89–96, 98, 100b, 100d, 100f, 100g**.

## Update — watermark replaced by a perspective angle

Per Maj: drop the watermark (it made the ads look like "just the product with a
watermark") and instead **composite the design at a slight perspective ANGLE** so
a screenshot isn't a clean, flat, usable copy — the product isn't really
screenshot-able once foreshortened, so no watermark is needed.

- `MockupService` now applies a mild PIL **perspective transform** to the framed
  print (viewed off-axis, right edge foreshortened) and the desk flat-lay (rotated
  + top edge receding). The watermark tiling was removed.
- Perspective coefficients are solved with a small **pure-Python Gaussian
  elimination** (no numpy — it isn't installed in prod).
- Settings `LISTING_WATERMARK_TEXT` removed; `MOCKUP_USE_GENERATED_SCENES` kept.
- `test_step100g` [5] now asserts the mockups are **not** watermarked and the
  design is **angled** (non-horizontal top edge). Visually confirmed: framed art
  viewed at an angle on a wall, and a tilted page on a desk — both clearly
  foreshortened, no watermark.
